Readiness Doctrine

The FRARI framework, assessment lifecycle, trajectory model, and publication rules that govern every readiness determination.

On This Page

01

Readiness Philosophy

Readiness is not a feeling. It is not a reputation. It is not a credential issued by proximity to the right institution. Readiness is a pattern — observable, documentable, and computable.

The BRSA readiness model rejects subjective assessment entirely. Every readiness determination is produced by applying a versioned ruleset to a structured evidence record. The same evidence, evaluated by the same ruleset, produces the same result every time. This is the foundation of a credible standard.

"Readiness is deterministic. If your method produces different results under the same conditions, you do not have a method — you have an opinion."
02

FRARI Ruleset

FRARI — the Foundational Readiness and Accreditation Ruleset for Individuals — is the scoring engine at the core of BRSA assessments. It is versioned, published, and applied consistently across all individual assessments.

03

Trajectory Model

Beyond a point-in-time score, BRSA tracks readiness trajectory — the directional movement of a score across multiple assessments over time.

Stable
Score 60 or above. Consistent pattern of engagement and documentation. Trajectory is maintained or improving.
Developing
Score 30–59. Active engagement with the process. Evidence collection underway. Trajectory is being established.
At Risk
Score below 30. Insufficient evidence, disengagement signals, or pattern inconsistency. Immediate intervention recommended.
04

Readiness Indicators

Five canonical indicators are evaluated in every BRSA assessment. Each is rated independently and contributes to the composite readiness score.

  • Documentation Discipline: The consistency, completeness, and timeliness of the subject's own record-keeping and filing practices.
  • Consent Compliance: Adherence to consent protocols — granting, maintaining, and not revoking consent without cause.
  • Evidence Completeness: The degree to which the submitted evidence record addresses all required assessment domains.
  • Pattern Consistency: Observable regularity in behavior across different contexts and time periods.
  • Escalation Integrity: The appropriate and timely escalation of issues, conflicts, or anomalies to the appropriate authority.
05

Assessment Lifecycle

Every BRSA assessment passes through a defined lifecycle. No assessment may skip a stage. Each stage is logged to the immutable ledger with a timestamp and actor.

1
Registration
Participant enters the registry. Consent is recorded. Registry ID is issued.
2
Assessment Requested
Evaluator or participant requests a readiness assessment. Window and ruleset version are specified.
3
Evidence Collection
Evaluator gathers and submits structured evidence across all five indicator domains.
4
Score Computed
FRARI ruleset applied to the evidence record. Score and trajectory are generated deterministically.
5
Published to Registry
Public readiness state is updated. Internal snapshot is sealed. Ledger entry is permanent.
06

Publication Rules

BRSA applies strict data minimalism to every public record. The following rules govern what is published and what remains internal:

  • Public fields: Registry ID, readiness state (RED/YELLOW/GREEN), status, last updated timestamp.
  • Internal only: Raw score, trajectory label, individual indicator ratings, evidence record, ledger audit trail, subject number, participant ID.
  • Never published: PII of any kind, contact information, financial data, legal documents, or any field not explicitly listed as public.
  • Immutability: Once a readiness snapshot is written to the ledger, it cannot be modified or deleted. Only a new assessment can change the public state.
