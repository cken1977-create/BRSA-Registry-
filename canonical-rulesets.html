Overview

The BRSA Standards Authority maintains three canonical rulesets — one for each assessment domain on the Legacyline platform. Each ruleset defines the scoring inputs, weights, normalization rules, and output format for its domain. Rulesets are versioned and permanently archived. A ruleset may never be modified after it is published — changes require a new version.

All three rulesets share the same foundational architecture: weighted composite scoring, deterministic computation, SHA-256 integrity sealing, and two-tier evaluator sign-off. They differ in their domains, input sources, and subject types.

Ruleset Governance

All three canonical rulesets are governed by the BRSA Standards Authority under the following principles:

I
Immutability
A published ruleset may never be modified. Changes require a new version with a new document ID. All prior versions remain permanently accessible.
II
Version Locking
Every assessment record stores the ruleset version used at computation time. A record may only be reproduced under the same version. Cross-version comparison is not permitted.
III
Public Availability
All canonical rulesets are published publicly at brsaregistry.com. No ruleset is proprietary. Transparency is a foundational requirement of the BRSA standard.
IV
Authority
Only the BRSA Standards Authority may publish, version, or deprecate a canonical ruleset. No platform implementation, evaluator, or institutional partner has authority to modify a ruleset.
