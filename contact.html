01

The Evaluator Role

BRSA evaluators are the practitioners of the standard. They conduct assessments, gather evidence, apply the FRARI ruleset, and publish findings to the registry. The evaluator role is the most consequential position in the BRSA ecosystem — every readiness determination that reaches the public record passes through an accredited evaluator.

Evaluators are not employees of BRSA. They are accredited practitioners who have demonstrated competence with the standard, committed to the governance framework, and accepted the ethical obligations of the role.

"An evaluator's signature on a readiness finding is a statement of professional and institutional integrity. We treat it accordingly."
02

Eligibility Requirements

BRSA evaluator accreditation is not open to everyone. The following baseline requirements apply to all applicants during the pilot phase:

  • Demonstrated professional background in behavioral assessment, social work, case management, counseling, legal advocacy, or a related discipline
  • Familiarity with structured documentation and evidence standards
  • No active conflicts of interest with subjects you would assess
  • Willingness to complete the BRSA evaluator training program
  • Commitment to the BRSA governance framework and publication rules

Institutional partners — organizations that wish to integrate BRSA assessments into their service delivery — have a separate pathway. Include your organization's name and mission in your submission.

04

Governance & Compliance Expectations

  • All assessments must be conducted in accordance with the current published FRARI ruleset
  • Evidence must be gathered with the explicit, documented consent of the subject
  • Findings may not be published to the registry without completing the full assessment lifecycle
  • Evaluators must recuse themselves from any assessment where a conflict of interest exists
  • Violations of the governance framework result in immediate suspension of accreditation pending review
05

Accreditation Pathway

1
Application Review
BRSA reviews your background statement and eligibility. Response within 5 business days.
2
Evaluator Training
Completion of the BRSA evaluator training program, covering the FRARI ruleset, assessment lifecycle, and publication rules.
3
Supervised Assessment
Completion of at least one supervised assessment reviewed by a senior BRSA evaluator.
4
Accreditation Issued
Evaluator accreditation is issued. You are authorized to conduct independent assessments and publish findings to the registry.
